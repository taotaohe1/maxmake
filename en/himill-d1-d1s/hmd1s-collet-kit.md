## 2. Why Do You Need This Expansion Kit? (Advanced Users Must Read)

Many beginners frequently remove the spindle nut to change collets of different sizes, which is not only inefficient but also repeatedly wears the spindle threads. The **"Large-diameter collet + reduction sleeve" solution** is the industrial-grade optimal choice.

### 2.1 "Set-it-and-forget-it" Quick Tool Change Process

By using the 6mm collet as the "permanent base" on the spindle, combined with reduction sleeves, you can quickly adapt by simply inserting/removing reduction sleeves without removing the collet nut:

- **6mm tools**: Direct clamping, ideal for roughing, provides maximum rigidity.
- **4mm tools**: Suitable for medium-sized end mills.
- **3.175mm (1/8") tools**: Compatible with a vast array of standard finishing tools.

**Advantage**: Significantly reduces process changeover time and minimizes spindle wear.

### 2.2 Perfect Compatibility with 3D Probe

Finding the workpiece origin is crucial before precision machining. MAXMAKE's **3D Touch Probe (Digital Probe)** typically uses a standard shank diameter.

Using the **6mm to 3.175mm reduction sleeve**, you can instantly mount the precision probe into the 6mm base.

This combination ensures probe mounting concentricity, elevating automatic tool setting and surface mapping (Auto-leveling) precision to the micron level.

### 2.3 Enhanced Heavy Cutting Stability

When cutting 6061 aluminum alloy or deep slotting in hardwood, 3.175mm shank tools tend to deflect or vibrate. Using 6mm shank tools with this collet kit significantly improves **cutting rigidity**, eliminates resonance, and achieves "mirror-like" surface finish.

## 5. Expert Precautions (⚠️ Must Read)

> ⚠️ **Safety Reminder**
> - **No Empty Locking**: Never forcefully tighten the collet without inserting a tool or reduction sleeve, as this will cause permanent plastic deformation damage to the elastic collet.
> - **Stop Before Operation**: All installation and removal must be done after the machine is completely powered off and the spindle has fully stopped.
> - **Insertion Depth**: Tool shank insertion depth should be at least 80% of the collet's total length to ensure even clamping force distribution.
> {.is-warning}

> 💡 **Maintenance Tips**
> - **Cleanliness is Key**: Tiny metal particles between the reduction sleeve and collet can exponentially amplify runout error. Use compressed air or lint-free cloth to clean during each tool change.
> - **3D Probe Protection**: When using the reduction sleeve to hold a digital probe, be careful not to overtighten - just ensure the probe doesn't wobble to avoid compressing the delicate probe shank.
> - **Regular Replacement**: Collets and reduction sleeves are precision consumables. If you notice rougher surface finish or unusual noise, check for metal fatigue or wear and replace the kit promptly.
> {.is-info}
