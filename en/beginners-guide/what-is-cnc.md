## 🤔 What Exactly is CNC?

CNC stands for **Computer Numerical Control**, simply put, it's **a technology that uses computers to control machines**.

Imagine this:
- If you want to carve a pattern on a piece of wood, the traditional method is to use hand tools (like a carving knife) to slowly carve it
- With CNC, the method is: first draw the pattern on a computer, then let the computer control the machine to automatically complete the carving

In other words, CNC makes machines "smart" - they can work automatically according to computer programs without manual operation throughout the process.

## 🏭 The Origin of CNC

The development of CNC technology dates back to the mid-20th century:

- **1940s**: During World War II, the U.S. Air Force needed to manufacture high-precision aircraft parts, and traditional manual methods couldn't meet the demand
- **1952**: The first CNC milling machine was born, developed by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and Parsons Corporation
- **1960s**: The development of computer technology made CNC more practical and began to be widely used in industry
- **1980s**: The popularity of personal computers made CNC equipment smaller and cheaper
- **21st century**: Desktop CNC equipment appeared, and ordinary people could use CNC technology for creation

## 📚 Types of CNC

CNC technology can be applied to many different types of machines. Here are the most common ones:

### 3D Printers

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/3d.jpg" alt="3D Printer" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Working principle**: Creates 3D objects by layering materials (such as plastic filament)
- **Applications**: Prototype production, personalized products, model making
- **Features**: Relatively low price, simple operation, suitable for beginners

### Laser Cutters

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/激光切割机.jpg" alt="Laser Cutter" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Working principle**: Uses high-energy laser beams to cut or engrave materials
- **Applications**: Advertising production, crafts, precision parts
- **Features**: High cutting precision, fast speed, suitable for thin materials

### CNC Engraving Machines

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/雕刻机.jpg" alt="CNC Engraving Machine" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />


- **Working principle**: Uses rotating tools to engrave patterns or text on material surfaces
- **Applications**: Personalized gifts, stamp making, sign making
- **Features**: Small size, suitable for desktop use, suitable for fine engraving

### CNC Milling Machines

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/铣床.jpg" alt="CNC Milling Machine" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Working principle**: The tool rotates, the workpiece is fixed, and the tool moves on the workpiece for cutting
- **Applications**: Mechanical parts, mold manufacturing, aerospace components
- **Features**: Powerful, can process multiple materials, high precision

### CNC Lathes

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/车床.jpg" alt="CNC Lathe" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Working principle**: The workpiece rotates, the tool is fixed, and the tool moves on the workpiece for cutting
- **Applications**: Shaft parts, thread processing, circular parts
- **Features**: Specifically used for processing circular or cylindrical workpieces

### Turning Centers

- **Working principle**: Combines the functions of lathes and milling machines, the workpiece can rotate, and the tool can also rotate
- **Applications**: Processing of complex parts, reducing processes
- **Features**: Comprehensive functions, high processing efficiency

## 🎯 CNC Axis Count

You may often hear terms like "3-axis CNC", "4-axis CNC", "5-axis CNC". What does this mean?

Simply put, **the number of axes is the number of directions the CNC machine can move**.

### 3-Axis CNC

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/三轴.jpg" alt="3-Axis CNC" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Movement directions**: X-axis (left-right), Y-axis (front-back), Z-axis (up-down)
- **Features**: The most basic and common CNC equipment
- **Applications**: Most flat and simple 3D processing
- **Suitable for**: Beginners, cost-effective

### 4-Axis CNC

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/四轴.jpg" alt="4-Axis CNC" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Movement directions**: X, Y, Z axes + one rotation axis (usually A-axis)
- **Features**: Can process cylindrical or complex curved workpieces
- **Applications**: Engraving cylinders, processing complex parts
- **Suitable for**: Users with some experience

### 5-Axis CNC

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-cnc/五轴.jpg" alt="5-Axis CNC" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

- **Movement directions**: X, Y, Z axes + two rotation axes (usually A-axis and B-axis)
- **Features**: Can process workpieces from any angle, most powerful function
- **Applications**: Aerospace parts, precision molds, complex artworks
- **Suitable for**: Professional users, industrial applications
