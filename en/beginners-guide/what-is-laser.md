## üì° 1. Definition and Principles of Laser Equipment

### 1. Definition

üî¶ **What is Laser Equipment?**

Laser equipment is an industrial machine that uses laser as a processing "tool" to cut, engrave, mark, and weld various materials such as metal, plastic, and wood by controlling the laser's irradiation path and energy level.

> üí° **Core Features**:
> - Belongs to "non-contact processing"
> - The laser beam only illuminates the material surface during processing
> - No need for hard tools, molds, or other hard objects to contact the material
> - This is the biggest difference between it and traditional machine tools
> 
> {.is-info}

### 2. Core Principles

‚ö° **How Laser Equipment Works**

Simply put, the core of laser equipment is to create a beam of extremely powerful, highly directional intense light (laser), then focus this beam into an extremely small spot, and use the high energy of the spot to process materials.

#### Specific Working Process:

1. **Laser Generation**: The equipment applies electricity/energy to an internal "special medium" (such as optical fiber, gas), causing this medium to emit intense light with completely consistent frequency and direction‚Äîthis is laser

2. **Conduction and Focusing**: Through mirrors, focusing lenses and other accessories, the laser is conducted to the material surface and focused into an extremely small spot

3. **Energy Concentration**: The energy of this spot becomes highly concentrated, and when it irradiates the material, it can quickly melt, vaporize the material, or even directly burn a gap

4. **Path Control**: The equipment controls this spot to move on the material surface according to a preset path, completing operations such as cutting, engraving, and marking

> üåü **Core Characteristics of Laser**:

## ‚öôÔ∏è 3. Core Components and Functions of Laser Equipment

Laser equipment is an integrated machine composed of 6 core parts, each performing its own duties and indispensable.

### 1. Laser Generator

‚ù§Ô∏è **Popular Positioning: The "Heart" of the Equipment**

**Core Function**: The core component that generates laser. Without a laser generator, no laser can be created. Its quality directly determines the laser's energy level and stability, making it the most core part of laser equipment.

### 2. Optical System

üîç **Popular Positioning: The "Optical Path Manager + Focusing Lens" of the Equipment**

**Core Function**: Composed of reflectors, focusing lenses, laser heads, optical fibers and other accessories, it mainly does two things:
- Conduct the laser generated by the laser generator to the material surface without loss
- Focus the laser into an extremely small spot to highly concentrate energy and ensure processing precision

### 3. Control System

üß† **Popular Positioning: The "Brain" of the Equipment**

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-laser/ÊéßÂà∂Á≥ªÁªü.jpg" alt="Control System" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

**Core Function**: Composed of operation panels, computer software, and control modules, it is the core of human-machine interaction, mainly doing three things:
- Receive processing instructions (such as importing drawings to be cut/engraved)
- Adjust processing parameters (such as laser power, processing speed, spot movement path)
- Control laser on/off and mechanical structure movement, allowing the equipment to automatically process according to preset requirements

### 4. Mechanical Structure

üèóÔ∏è **Popular Positioning: The "Body + Worktable" of the Equipment**

**Core Function**: Composed of machine tool body, processing worktable, transmission components, and fixtures, it mainly does two things:
- Place the material to be processed (workpiece) and fix the workpiece firmly with fixtures
- Drive the workpiece or laser head to move precisely, ensuring the laser spot can accurately illuminate the designated position on the material according to the control system's instructions

### 5. Cooling System

‚ùÑÔ∏è **Popular Positioning: The "Heat Dissipation Fan / Water Chiller" of the Equipment**

**Core Function**: Composed of water chillers, heat dissipation fans, and cooling pipelines. When laser equipment works, the laser generator and optical system generate a lot of heat. Excessive temperature will directly burn out core components. The function of the cooling system is to cool these core components in real time to ensure the equipment operates at normal temperature.

> üí° **Cooling Method Selection**:
> - High-power laser equipment: Generally use water chillers for cooling
> - Low-power equipment: Heat dissipation fans are sufficient

### 6. Safety Protection System

üõ°Ô∏è **Popular Positioning: The "Safety Barrier" of the Equipment**

<img src="/eng/beginners-guide/media/what-is-laser/ÂÆâÂÖ®Èò≤Êä§Á≥ªÁªü.jpg" alt="Safety Protection System" style="width: 50%; height: auto; border-radius: 10px;" />

**Core Function**: Composed of protective covers, laser protective glasses, emergency stop buttons, sound and light alarms, etc., its core is to protect the safety of operators and equipment:
- Prevent direct laser beam irradiation to people, avoiding eye and skin burns
- Prevent molten slag and dust from splashing and hurting people during processing
- When equipment malfunctions (such as overheating, short circuit), it can quickly stop operation through emergency stop and sound-light alarm to prevent equipment damage

## üìä 5. Key Parameter Analysis of Laser Equipment

These three parameters determine what materials the equipment can process, how precise the processing can be, and how thick the materials can be processed‚Äîthey are the key to selecting equipment and adjusting processing parameters.

### 3. Peak Power

‚ö° **What is Peak Power?**

The maximum laser energy that laser equipment can output in a short time, not the power of the equipment during continuous operation‚Äîequivalent to the laser's "maximum strength that can be exerted instantly," which can be adjusted through the control system.

| Item | Description |
|------|------------|
| **Unit** | Watts (W), kilowatts (kW) |
| **Actual Impact** | Determines the laser's penetration ability and processing depth |

#### Core Correspondence:

| Peak Power | Penetration Ability | Suitable Scenarios | Typical Applications |
|------------|---------------------|-------------------|---------------------|

> Peak power is not the higher the better‚Äîit should be adjusted according to the thickness and hardness of the processed material. Too high power will burn and deform the material processing surface, affecting processing quality.
